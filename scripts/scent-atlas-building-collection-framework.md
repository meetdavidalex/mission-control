### INTRO (60 seconds)

**[HOOK — Use Hook 1]**

Here's the thing about fragrance collections — most people build them backwards. They see a bottle that looks cool, or they hear about something trending on TikTok, or they get a good deal at a department store sale. Six months later, they have 15 fragrances and still feel like they have nothing to wear.

I know because I did exactly that. My first year collecting, I bought everything. Freshies, ouds, gourmands, things I thought I "should" own. I probably spent close to five grand. And you know what? I wore maybe five of them regularly.

The turning point was when I stopped asking "does this smell good?" and started asking "where does this fit in my life?" That one question changed everything.

Today I'm going to walk you through how to build a fragrance collection that actually makes sense. Whether you're starting from zero or trying to fix a collection that's gotten out of hand, this framework will help. We're going to cover the essential categories, how to prioritize your purchases, and the mistakes that waste money.

Let's build something intentional.

### SECTION 1: The Framework — Why Most Collections Fail (90 seconds)

**[ON CAMERA]**

Before we talk about what to buy, let's talk about why most collections don't work.

**Problem one: no system.** People buy what catches their attention, not what fills a gap. You end up with five fresh citrus scents and nothing for date night. Or three heavy winter ouds and nothing for summer.

**Problem two: occasion blindness.** A fragrance doesn't exist in a vacuum. It exists in your life — at work, on dates, at the gym, running errands. If you don't know when you'll actually wear something, you won't wear it.

**Problem three: hype chasing.** Someone online says this is the best fragrance ever released. Maybe it is — for them. But their life isn't your life. Their climate isn't your climate. Their style isn't your style.

The solution is simple: build around situations, not scents. Think about where you actually go, what you actually do, and buy fragrances for those moments.

---

### SECTION 2: The Essential Categories (3 minutes)

**[ON CAMERA]**

Here are the five categories that cover 95% of situations. You don't need all five to start, but this is the complete framework.

**Category one: The Signature Scent.** This is your default. The one you reach for when you don't know what to wear. It should be versatile — work-appropriate but interesting, crowd-pleasing but not boring. Something you could wear 3-4 times a week and never get tired of. For me, this was Creed Aventus for years. Now it's often Diptyque Philosykos. Your signature might be different — the point is having one.

**Category two: The Professional Scent.** Office-appropriate, inoffensive, projects competence. This might overlap with your signature, or it might be separate if your signature is bolder. Think clean, not loud. Fresh or lightly woody. Nothing that enters the room before you do.

**Category three: The Date Night Scent.** This is where you can take more risks. Something sensual, interesting, memorable. It doesn't need to please everyone — it needs to please the person you're close to. This is often where people go sweeter, spicier, or more animalic.

**Category four: The Casual/Weekend Scent.** Easy, relaxed, probably lighter than your other choices. Something for running errands, brunch with friends, weekend activities. This is often where fresh, aquatic, or citrus scents live.

**Category five: The Special Occasion Scent.** The expensive one. The bold one. The one you save for events where you want to make an impression. This might be your niche investment, your oud, your statement fragrance. You won't wear it often, but when you do, it matters.

### SECTION 3: Building From Zero — The Starter Collection (3 minutes)

**[ON CAMERA]**

If you're starting from scratch, here's my recommended order of operations:

**Bottle one: Your signature.** Spend real time on this. Test extensively. This is the foundation everything else builds around. Budget: whatever you can afford, but don't cheap out. You'll wear this more than anything else.

**Bottle two: Contrast to your signature.** If your signature is fresh, go warm. If it's light, go deeper. This gives you options. If you chose a versatile fresh scent first, your second might be a date night fragrance. If you went with something bold, get something professional.

**Bottle three: Fill the biggest gap.** Look at your life. Where do you spend time? What's missing? If you work in an office, maybe you need that professional scent. If you're dating, maybe it's time for something sensual. If you live somewhere hot, maybe you need a summer-specific option.

**Bottles four and five: Specialization.** By now you know what you like and what you need. Maybe you want a dedicated gym scent. Maybe you want something for formal events. Maybe you want to explore a specific note — oud, tobacco, leather. This is where personal preference takes over.

**[B-ROLL: Visual progression showing collection growing from 1 to 5 bottles]**

The key insight: don't buy bottle three until you've lived with bottles one and two for a while. You need to understand what you're actually reaching for, not what you think you'll wear.

### SECTION 4: Common Mistakes That Waste Money (2.5 minutes)

**[ON CAMERA]**

Let me save you some expensive lessons.

**Mistake one: buying full bottles before testing.** I know, I know — you want the bottle on your shelf. But a $5 sample can save you a $200 mistake. Most niche houses sell discovery sets. Use them. Live with a fragrance for a few days before committing.

**Mistake two: ignoring your climate.** I live in Southern California. I can wear fresh scents year-round. If you live somewhere with real winters, you need different options. If you live in humidity, heavy scents become suffocating. Buy for where you live, not where you vacation.

**Mistake three: buying for fantasy you.** That oud you love in the store — when are you actually going to wear it? That super-niche artisan fragrance — do you have anywhere to wear it? Be honest about your life.

**Mistake four: neglecting performance.** A fragrance that disappears in two hours isn't worth the money, no matter how good it smells. Check reviews for longevity and projection before you buy.

**Mistake five: collecting instead of curating.** At some point, more isn't better. I hit that point around 30 bottles. Now I'm actively downsizing. Every fragrance should earn its place. If you haven't worn it in six months, sell it or give it away.

Let me give you three concrete examples for different situations:

### SECTION 6: Maintenance and Evolution (1.5 minutes)

**[ON CAMERA]**

Your collection isn't static. It should evolve as you do.

**Quarterly review:** Look at what you actually wore. If something sat untouched, ask why. Wrong season? Wrong occasion? Or just not "you" anymore? Be ruthless.

**One in, one out:** When you buy something new, consider selling something old. Keeps the collection manageable and funds the next purchase.

**Seasonal rotation:** Store off-season fragrances properly. Heat and light destroy perfume. Dark, cool places. Original boxes if you have them.

**Track your wears:** Sounds obsessive, but I started noting what I wore each day. Revealed patterns I didn't expect. Showed me what I was neglecting. Helped me understand my own preferences better.

If you're just starting out, my advice is simple: buy one really good fragrance you love, wear the hell out of it, and learn what you actually want before buying bottle two.
