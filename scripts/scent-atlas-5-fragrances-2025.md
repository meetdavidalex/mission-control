### THE LANDSCAPE OF 2025 (0:45 - 2:00)

"Before we get to the list, let me set the stage for what's happening in fragrance this year. Because understanding the trends helps you understand why these five matter.

2025 is the year fragrance went edible — but not in the way you think. We're not talking about cotton candy body sprays from the early 2000s. This is sophisticated, nuanced, wearable food. Vanilla that smells like a leather armchair in a library. Citrus that feels like sunlight on skin. Nutty notes that somehow smell expensive instead of like a protein bar.

The other big shift? Unisex is the default now. The lines between 'men's' and 'women's' fragrances have basically dissolved. What matters is the scent, not the marketing. And honestly? That's how it always should have been.

Finally, we're seeing niche quality at designer prices. The gap between a $400 niche fragrance and a $150 designer release has never been smaller. Some of the best scents this year are from houses you've actually heard of.

### FRAGRANCE 1: CREED ABSOLU AVENTUS (2:00 - 4:00)

"First up: Creed Absolu Aventus. And yes, I know — Aventus is the most talked-about men's fragrance of the last decade. It's the scent that launched a thousand clones. But Absolu Aventus isn't just a flanker. It's a genuine evolution.

The original Aventus is pineapple, birch, smoke — bold, masculine, attention-grabbing. Absolu keeps the DNA but reimagines it. The pineapple is still there, but it's richer, deeper. There's more warmth, more complexity in the base. Think of it as Aventus for someone who doesn't need to announce their presence from across the room.

What I love about this fragrance is the wearability. Original Aventus can feel like a statement piece — you're wearing THE Aventus. Absolu is more versatile. It works in the office. It works on a date. It works at a casual weekend brunch. It's confident without trying too hard.

Longevity is excellent — I get 8-10 hours consistently. Projection is moderate. People will notice it, but they won't smell you before they see you. That's the sweet spot for me.

Is it expensive? Absolutely. This is Creed we're talking about. But if you've been curious about Aventus and found the original too loud or too common, Absolu might be exactly what you're looking for. It's the grown-up version."

### FRAGRANCE 2: DIOR BOIS TALISMAN (4:00 - 5:45)

"Next: Dior Bois Talisman. And this is where we talk about what vanilla became in 2025.

For years, vanilla was the note you avoided if you wanted to smell sophisticated. It was synthetic, sweet, juvenile. The fragrance equivalent of a cupcake. But perfumers have completely reinvented vanilla this year, and Bois Talisman is the best example I've found.

This isn't sweet vanilla. This is vanilla wrapped in smoke, woods, and something that smells like old books and leather. It's warm without being gourmand. It's inviting without being cloying. When I wear this, I feel like I'm sitting in a private library with a fireplace and a really good whiskey.

The opening has a brightness to it — there's citrus in there that keeps it from feeling too heavy. But the drydown is where the magic happens. The vanilla becomes this rich, creamy backdrop for woods and resins. It's complex. It evolves over hours. And it lasts — I'm talking 10+ hours on skin, longer on clothes.

Here's what surprised me: I get more compliments wearing this than almost anything else in my collection. And not just from women — from men who want to know what it is so they can buy it. That's the mark of a truly great unisex fragrance.

At $2,550, the price is absurd. I know. But Dior's private collection is an investment, and this is one of the best releases they've had in years. If you can get a sample, do it. If you can swing the bottle, you won't regret it."

### FRAGRANCE 3: D.S. & DURGA GRAPEFRUIT GENERATION (5:45 - 7:15)

"Third: D.S. & Durga Grapefruit Generation. This is citrus done right in 2025.

Citrus fragrances are usually the most boring category. They smell like cleaning products or cheap body spray. They last 20 minutes and then you're wearing nothing. But Grapefruit Generation breaks every rule.

The grapefruit here is real — tart, juicy, slightly bitter. It smells like you just peeled a grapefruit in the sun. But what makes this special is what happens underneath. There's depth here. Woods, herbs, something green and earthy that keeps it grounded.

This is a summer fragrance that doesn't feel disposable. It has personality. It has presence. And somehow — miraculously for a citrus scent — it actually lasts. I'm getting 6-7 hours, which is unheard of for this category.

I wear this when I want to feel fresh but not basic. When it's 90 degrees and I still want to smell like I have taste. It's become my go-to for travel, for weekends, for any time I want something effortless that still makes a statement.

At $130, this is an absolute steal. D.S. & Durga is a niche house with mainstream accessibility. Their stuff is creative, high-quality, and fairly priced. Grapefruit Generation might be their best release this year."

### FRAGRANCE 5: LELABO THÉ MATCHA 26 (8:45 - 10:15)

"Finally: Le Labo Thé Matcha 26. This is the wildcard. The unexpected scent that shouldn't work but absolutely does.

Matcha in a fragrance sounds like a gimmick. It sounds like something that would smell like a Starbucks or a health food store. But Le Labo — as they always do — found the sophistication in it.

This is green, grassy, and slightly bitter in the opening. It smells like a Japanese tea ceremony in a bottle. But as it dries down, it becomes creamy, smooth, almost milky. There's fig in here that adds sweetness without making it gourmand. There's woods that ground everything.

What I love about this fragrance is how different it is from everything else on the market. You won't smell like anyone else. You won't remind people of their ex-boyfriend or their dad or that guy at the gym. This is a signature scent in the truest sense — it's distinctive, memorable, completely its own thing.

Longevity is solid — 7-8 hours. Projection is moderate. It's not a beast mode fragrance, but it doesn't need to be. This is about quality over quantity.

At $340, it's expensive. Le Labo always is. But you're paying for craftsmanship, for uniqueness, for a scent that nobody else is wearing. If you want to stand out without being loud, this is how you do it."

### THE TRENDS THAT MATTER (10:15 - 11:15)

"Let's step back and talk about what these five fragrances tell us about 2025.

First: vanilla is back, but it's grown up. The sweet, synthetic vanillas of the past are being replaced by complex, sophisticated interpretations. If you wrote off vanilla years ago, it's time to reconsider.

Second: citrus got interesting. It's not just lemon and bergamot anymore. Grapefruit, yuzu, blood orange — perfumers are exploring the full spectrum, and they're adding depth and longevity that citrus usually lacks.

Third: edible doesn't mean gourmand. Nutty notes, tea notes, even savory elements are appearing in wearable, sophisticated ways. Fragrance is expanding beyond the traditional categories.

Fourth: unisex is the new normal. The best fragrances this year don't care about gender. They care about smelling incredible on whoever wears them.

And finally: quality is democratizing. You can find exceptional fragrances at $70 or $2,500. The price doesn't always correlate with the experience anymore."
